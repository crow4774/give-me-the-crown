Myths vs. Facts

Let's take a closer look at how popular culture as created certain myths surrounding how crime is solved and tried in court. We will focus on research into the real processes.


Myth #1
Forensic scientists rarely work homicide cases, consideringa very small portion of the population actually gets murdered.

Fact #1
Fingerprints aren't actually foolproof. While they can be unique to each person, it is often difficult to find enough unique characteristics to identify a specific individual. Even highly trained experts have a hard time identifying prints.

More Info


Fact #2
DNA has been incredibly useful in exonerating wrongfully-convicted indivduals. It has helped save many innocent people from spending anymore time behind bars, and even getting some off of death-row.

More Info


Fact #3
The proof is always in the bones...well in teeth. Dental records identify over 90% of individuals. And it is a heck of a lot faster than DNA. Another fun fact is that bones, hair, nails, and teeth are the last to decay.
